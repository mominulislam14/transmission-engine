Brake System Service

A well maintained brake system plays an important role for safe driving; therefore, it is essential to check you brakes as soon as possible if you experience any related problems.

Transmission 4 less will help you troubleshoot your problem and repair it in a short time frame at the lowest price in town! We provide full repair service for your vehicles brake system needs!

Brake Warning Signs:

  • Noise is one of the leading indicators that something is wrong with your vehicle brakes.
  • Some of the noises associated with a poor performance of your brakes are: whistling,squeaking, grinding or pulsing.
  • When brakes are close to their useful life, the driver is forced to press harder on the pedal to make the vehicle stop.
  • When pressure is applied to the brakes, the vehicle has a tendency to pull to one side.

Easy to follow tips to extend the life of your brakes:

  • Go easy on the speed: Stops from high speed is the most important factor that could reduce the life of your brakes.
  • Stop overloading your vehicle with too much freight. Remember that a heavy vehicle is harder on brakes
  • Avoid unnecessary braking.
  • Do not haul the brakes while accelerating
  • Renew your brake fluid constantly, especially if you have an older vehicle

A/C Services

Air conditioning facilitates the removal of heat and humidity from inside the vehicle. Powered by a belt, the air conditioning compressor compresses the refrigerant to pressurize it and pump it through the system. To maintain the efficiency of the air conditioning system the compressor drive belt should be checked regularly. If it shows signs of deterioration, it is important to replace it as soon as possible in order to ensure a smooth A/C run.

Simple maintenance tips for a smooth A/C run:

  • Make sure your unit is not leaking and is draining as it should.
  • Change the filter every 4-6 weeks.
  • Clean off the outside air compressor with water.

Shocks and Struts Repair

The main characteristic of shocks absorbers and struts is to influence the control and handling characteristics of your vehicle. In other words, the shocks and struts absorbers controls movement when a car is driven into a corner or over bumps. These units are comprised of shock oil, dampening valves, a piston, main seal, main body with mounting bushing and an activation shaft with mounting bushing.

Signs of worn shocks or struts:

  • Excessive bounce when crossing and intersection
  • Rocking back and forth when stopping your vehicle quickly
  • Vehicle drifting left or right when applying brakes at high speed
  • Bending from side to side when changing lines rather quickly

Fuel Injection Service

A fuel injection system is used to inject atomized fuel directly into the cylinders of an internal-combustion engine. Nowadays, fuel injection cleaning is a required maintenance service on vehicles. You should consider a fuel injection service when your vehicle is hesitating, having trouble starting, achieving poor gas mileage or operating sluggish with poor performance.

Steps for a proper fuel injector system service:

  • Check fuel pump operating pressure and volume.
  • Test pressure regulator for operation and leakage.
  • Flush entire fuel rail and upper fuel injector screens to include pressure regulator.
  • Clean fuel injectors.
  • Decarbon engine assembly.
  • Clean throttle plate and idle air control (IAC) passages.
  • Check minimum air flow rate and adjust if needed.
  • Relearn onboard computer.

After this service, your vehicle will achieve optimum fuel efficiency, and it should show signs of a better response and performance

A failed water pump will result in the overheating of the engine, which might cause serious problems to it. Noises from the engine or small leaks are some of the indicators of a failed water pump

Car Care Services

Proper engine maintenance is the key to your vehicle’s long and useful life. Here are some tips to keep your vehicle’s engine in good shape:

  • Regular Oil Changes: It is crucial to your vehicle’s performance. The recommended oil change is between 3,000 to 6,000 miles.
  • Air Filter: Dirt and other particles should be kept away in order to prevent them from entering the system and possible damaging the engine.
  • Car Battery: Keeping your battery in great shape is crucial to the overall functioning of your engine.
  • Spark Plugs: Removing defective spark plugs is important for an optimum performance of the engine. New spark plugs will
    help your vehicle start easier and operate more efficiently.
  • Timing Belt: If the timing belt looks like it reached its useful life, you might need to replace it with a new one; otherwise,
    it will slow down the efficiency of the vehicle engine..
  • Original Engine Parts: Faulty engine components could damage the engine completely.
  • Car Fluids: The brake fluid, engine fluid, engine coolant, power steering fluid, and transmission fluid should be filled to the marked level in order to avoid malfunctioning.

These are small maintenance costs that could prevent expensive damage , your car deserves care !

Power Steering Maintenance

The power steering system is a device driven by the engine of a vehicle that facilitates the turning of the steering wheel by the driver. The power steering system is made up of numerous components that must be checked constantly because they might fail after several years of use. A leak is most likely to be the obvious sign that you are having problems with the vehicle’s power steering system. Also, a leak might suggest that your vehicle is low on power steering fluid. Therefore, it is important to check the vehicle’s fluids regularly, so you could avoid other related problems.

Timing Belts Replacement

A broken engine belt could be quite costly to the driver’s wallet, therefore it is essential to replace it at the right time. Your vehicle owner’s manual will recommend at what mileage the timing belt must be replaced. These intervals range from every 60,000 miles to every 105,000 miles.

Exhaust and Mufflers Replacement

A muffler is a device that reduces the noise emitted by your vehicle. The noise is formed from pulses of alternating high and low air pressure. Mufflers are typically installed along the exhaust pipe as part of the exhaust system of an internal combustion engine to reduce its exhaust noise.
